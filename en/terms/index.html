General contractual terms for B2B engineering services

June 5, 2026 · English

This page contains Fovebo’s general contractual terms for B2B engineering services. Client-specific Order Forms, service descriptions and annexes may apply in addition and take precedence in case of express deviation.

The English version is provided for convenience and website consistency. Unless expressly agreed otherwise in a client-specific Order Form, the German version governs.

Fovebo General Terms

General contractual terms for B2B engineering services

1. Scope and incorporation

(1) These Fovebo General Terms apply to all B2B services provided by Fovebo LLC (“Fovebo”) to entrepreneurs, legal entities under public law or special funds under public law (“Client”), to the extent they are incorporated into an offer, individual agreement, Order Form, service description or other agreement.

(2) These Terms do not apply to consumers.

(3) Individual agreements, in particular a signed offer, Order Form, Statement of Work or client-specific service description, take precedence over these Terms.

(4) General terms and conditions, purchasing terms or other contractual terms of the Client apply only if Fovebo expressly agrees to their application in text form. Silence, performance of services or acceptance of payments does not constitute consent.

(5) Fovebo may use different offer formats, in particular retainers, flex contingents, workshops, audits, packaged services or project-based implementations. The specific offer format is determined by the relevant individual agreement.

2. Contract conclusion and contract documents

(1) A contract is concluded by signature, electronic signature, express acceptance of an offer in text form or by commencement of service performance after mutual coordination.

(2) The specific scope of services, fees, payment method, term, notice periods, capacity, dates, contacts, communication channels, access rights and other project-specific details are determined by the relevant individual agreement.

(3) Where annexes or modules are used, they may include in particular a Security Baseline/TOMs, Data Access Boundaries, a data processing agreement, standard contractual clauses or offboarding confirmations.

(4) In case of contradictions, the following order of precedence applies:

(a) individually negotiated agreement,

(b) Order Form / offer / service description,

(c) data protection or SCC annexes for data protection matters,

(d) these General Terms,

(e) further modules or annexes, unless otherwise agreed.

3. Service model

(1) Fovebo provides services in the areas of software development, backend engineering, PostgreSQL performance, technical analysis, stabilization, maintenance, consulting, code review, automation, tooling, documentation or related technical services.

(2) The services are generally services. A specific result, a specific work product or the achievement of a specific economic outcome is not owed unless expressly agreed otherwise.

(3) Tickets, backlogs, pull requests, repositories, documentation systems, time tracking, meetings or sprints serve coordination, transparency and collaboration. They do not establish organizational integration of Fovebo into the Client’s operations.

(4) Fovebo does not owe product responsibility, project management, on-call duty, 24/7 availability, client representation or other tasks outside the expressly agreed scope of services.

4. Remote work, place of work and collaboration

(1) Fovebo generally provides services remotely from Paraguay. The Client is aware that service performance does not take place physically within the EU/EEA unless expressly agreed otherwise.

(2) Collaboration is generally remote and async-first. Alignment, questions and coordination take place through the agreed communication channels and, where appropriate, within agreed overlap windows with the Client’s teams.

(3) An agreed overlap window serves collaboration and coordination. It does not establish a duty of permanent availability, employee-like working time, on-call duty or a presence obligation.

(4) If the Client has internal policies requiring physical work performance within the EU/EEA or at a specific location, the Client must communicate this before contract conclusion. If such a policy is mandatory, collaboration with Fovebo may not be possible or may be possible only with a different structure.

5. Independence, no employee leasing

(1) The parties act as legally independent companies. These Terms and the relevant individual agreement do not establish an employment relationship, partnership, commercial agency relationship or other representation relationship.

(2) Fovebo provides the services on its own responsibility and is not organizationally integrated into the Client’s operations. Fovebo independently determines place of work, work organization, approach, methods and internal service performance.

(3) Instructions from the Client are limited to technical requirements, priorities, security requirements, cooperation, approvals and scheduling coordination within the agreed scope of services. Employment-law instructions do not exist.

(4) The parties agree that no employee leasing is intended. No employees are leased to the Client.

(5) The Client has no claim to the deployment of specific persons unless this is expressly set out in the individual agreement.

6. No authority to represent and no client representation

(1) Fovebo is not authorized to legally represent the Client, conclude contracts in the Client’s name, promise prices or commercial terms to third parties or otherwise legally bind the Client.

(2) Communication with customers, suppliers or other third parties of the Client takes place only to the extent required for technical coordination or expressly coordinated. This does not include commercial or legal representation.

(3) Fovebo will not create the impression toward third parties that it is an employee, corporate body, commercial agent or legal representative of the Client.

7. Client cooperation obligations

(1) The Client provides all information, documents, decisions, contacts, access, roles, permissions, test data, documentation, approvals and other prerequisites required for service performance in good time.

(2) Cooperation requests from Fovebo must be processed within a reasonable time. To the extent cooperation acts are not performed in time, dates, priorities and schedules shift accordingly; further rights of Fovebo remain unaffected.

(3) Security, data protection, compliance, purchasing or other internal requirements of the Client must be communicated to Fovebo in text form before the affected service begins. They apply only to the extent they are reasonable, proportionate and compatible with the agreed scope of services.

(4) Delays, additional effort or performance obstacles caused by missing, late or withdrawn cooperation by the Client are not attributable to Fovebo and do not create an automatic obligation to reperform or transfer capacity free of charge.

8. Work equipment, client systems and access

(1) Fovebo generally uses its own professional work equipment unless the Client requires specific work equipment or client devices for security, compliance or organizational reasons.

(2) If the Client requires the use of specific work equipment, in particular a secured device, the Client provides it in operational condition and is responsible for provisioning, access, updates, support and return.

(3) Provided work equipment remains the property of the Client. Fovebo uses it exclusively for contract performance and returns it after contract end or upon request.

(4) Access to client systems may be used exclusively for service performance. The Client remains responsible for provision, technical functionality, role/rights management and revocation of access.

9. Access, data and security model

(1) Access, permissions and data should be structured according to the principle of least privilege.

(2) Where technically possible, analysis, development, testing and troubleshooting are performed on the basis of test, staging, anonymized, pseudonymized or otherwise risk-reduced data.

(3) Access to production systems or personal production data occurs only to the extent required for service performance, approved by the Client and traceable.

(4) Fovebo does not create local copies of personal or otherwise confidential client data unless this is required for service performance or expressly approved.

(5) Client data no longer required must be deleted or returned after contract end or upon request in accordance with contractual and statutory requirements.

(6) Fovebo’s general security baseline may be agreed or referenced as an optional module. Project-specific access boundaries may be documented in a Data Access Boundary document.

(7) If additional security or compliance requirements of the Client lead to significant additional effort, the parties will agree on an appropriate adjustment of dates, capacity or fees.

10. Confidentiality

(1) The parties undertake not to disclose confidential information of the other party to third parties and to use it exclusively for contractual purposes.

(2) Confidential information means all information marked as confidential or confidential by its nature, in particular trade secrets, technical information, source code, access credentials, security information, client data, prices, strategies, internal documentation and non-public project information.

(3) The confidentiality obligation does not apply to information that is publicly known, becomes publicly known without breach of contract, was already lawfully known to the receiving party, was lawfully disclosed by a third party or was independently developed without use of confidential information.

(4) The confidentiality obligation continues after contract end.

11. Data protection and third-country access

(1) The parties clarify on a project-specific basis whether and to what extent personal data is processed and which data protection roles apply.

(2) If Fovebo processes personal data on behalf of the Client, the parties will conclude a data processing agreement (DPA) before the relevant processing begins or use a suitable client template.

(3) To the extent service performance involves access from Paraguay or another third country outside the EU/EEA and appropriate safeguards are required for this, the parties will separately agree the necessary additional arrangements, in particular standard contractual clauses or comparable transfer documentation.

(4) Fovebo reports data protection or security incidents that may affect personal data or confidential information of the Client to the Client without undue delay after becoming aware of them, where possible within twenty-four (24) hours.

(5) The parties cooperate to a reasonable extent in containing, investigating, documenting and remediating such incidents.

12. Subcontractors and other third parties

(1) The use of project-related subcontractors or other third parties for service performance requires the Client’s prior consent in text form. Consent may not be unreasonably withheld if the Client’s legitimate security, confidentiality or data protection interests are preserved.

(2) Fovebo remains responsible for service performance and compliance with contractual obligations.

(3) General service providers of Fovebo that do not provide project-related services and do not receive access to client systems or client data are not considered project-related subcontractors. Data protection requirements for subprocessors remain unaffected.

13. Rights to work results and pre-existing rights

(1) Client-specific work results that Fovebo creates specifically for the Client within the agreed services transfer to the Client, after full payment of the respectively due fees, as exclusive, temporally and geographically unrestricted rights of use to the extent legally transferable and unless otherwise agreed.

(2) Pre-existing tools, libraries, templates, generic components, routines, frameworks, scripts, documentation building blocks, working methods and other know-how of Fovebo remain the property or rights portfolio of Fovebo.

(3) To the extent such pre-existing components are an integral part of a work result, the Client receives a simple, non-exclusive right of use to them to the extent required for contractual use.

(4) Fovebo may continue to use general know-how, methods, experience, concepts and solution approaches for other clients, provided no confidential information of the Client is disclosed and no client-specific work results are used without authorization.

(5) Open-source software and other third-party components are subject exclusively to their respective license terms. Fovebo is not obligated to transfer rights to such third-party components.

(6) Until full payment of the respectively due fees, the Client receives only a provisional, revocable right of use to work results within the scope of the contractually intended use.

14. Fees, invoicing and payment

(1) Fees, currency, billing cycle, payment term, retainer capacity, hourly rates, daily rates, package prices or other remuneration are determined by the relevant individual agreement.

(2) All amounts are, where applicable, exclusive of statutory VAT or comparable charges.

(3) Payments must be made without deduction of bank fees, withholding taxes or other withholdings unless such deduction is legally mandatory. If a statutory withholding is required, the Client informs Fovebo in advance and provides suitable evidence of the withholding.

(4) The parties support each other to a reasonable extent in providing required tax certificates, company evidence or forms. VAT treatment, in particular reverse-charge or comparable procedures, is governed by the respectively applicable tax rules.

(5) Additional services outside the agreed scope are performed only after prior coordination and approval in text form and are compensated according to the agreed rates or a separate agreement.

15. Retainers, contingents and unused capacity

(1) Where a retainer or hours/days contingent is agreed, it serves to reserve agreed capacity of Fovebo.

(2) The retainer is owed regardless of whether the Client uses the full capacity, unless the individual agreement provides otherwise.

(3) Carry-over, expiry, caps, pauses, minimum terms and special rules for unused capacity are determined by the relevant individual agreement.

(4) Fovebo is not obligated to provide more than the agreed capacity. Additional services require prior approval and are treated as additional services.

16. Change requests and additional requirements

(1) Changes to the scope of services, additional requirements, new systems, additional reports, extended security/compliance reviews, changed access routes, special documentation obligations or other deviations from the agreed scope are considered change requests unless they are already covered by the agreed scope of services.

(2) Change requests require coordination in text form. If they lead to additional effort, delay or changed risk, dates, capacity, priorities and fees may be adjusted appropriately.

(3) Fovebo is not obligated to implement change requests before corresponding approval.

17. Dates, delays and force majeure

(1) Dates and schedules are binding only if expressly agreed as binding. For services, schedules are regularly a planning and coordination basis.

(2) Delays due to missing cooperation, pending approvals, non-functional client systems, changed requirements or force majeure are not attributable to Fovebo.

(3) Force majeure includes in particular natural disasters, war, terrorism, pandemics, governmental orders, prolonged large-scale power, internet or infrastructure outages and other unforeseeable circumstances outside the affected party’s sphere of influence.

(4) If a force majeure event lasts longer than thirty (30) days, both parties are entitled to terminate the affected individual agreement for good cause.

18. Payment default and suspension of services

(1) If the Client is in default with a due invoice, Fovebo is entitled, after reminder and expiry of a grace period of five (5) business days, to suspend service performance until full receipt of payment.

(2) A suspension does not affect the term of the affected individual agreement.

(3) In case of payment default, statutory default interest and default lump sums apply where applicable.

(4) If the Client remains in default for longer than thirty (30) calendar days or payment default occurs repeatedly, Fovebo may terminate the affected individual agreement extraordinarily for good cause.

19. Term, termination and contract end

(1) Term, minimum term and ordinary termination are determined by the relevant individual agreement.

(2) The right to extraordinary termination for good cause remains unaffected.

(3) At contract end, outstanding fees, approved additional services, approved expenses and other accrued claims must be invoiced.

(4) Access, client data, work equipment, secrets, work results and offboarding measures are handled in accordance with the individual agreement, these Terms and any annexes.

(5) An Offboarding and Deletion Confirmation may be carried out using an optionally agreed module.

20. Liability

(1) Fovebo is liable in accordance with statutory provisions for intent and gross negligence as well as for damages arising from injury to life, body or health.

(2) In case of slightly negligent breach of material contractual obligations, liability is limited to the typical, foreseeable damage and, in amount, to the liability cap agreed in the individual agreement. If no liability cap is agreed, the maximum amount is the sum of the fees paid or owed for the affected individual agreement in the twelve (12) months before the event giving rise to liability.

(3) Otherwise, Fovebo’s liability for slight negligence is excluded. Liability for lost profit, unrealized savings and indirect consequential damages is excluded to the extent legally permissible.

(4) Mandatory statutory liability, in particular under product liability law or for fraudulently concealed defects, remains unaffected.

21. Reference use

(1) Fovebo may use the Client, its logo and a short factual description of the collaboration as a reference only with the Client’s prior consent in text form.

(2) The Client will not unreasonably withhold such consent provided no confidential information is disclosed and the Client’s legitimate interests are preserved.

(3) Confidential information, internal metrics, security details, personal data or other non-public information of the Client may not be disclosed as part of reference use.

22. Text form, electronic communication and signatures

(1) Where text form is agreed, this includes in particular email, electronic signature, written confirmation in an agreed tool or another permanently readable electronic declaration, provided the sender is identifiable.

(2) Electronic signatures and signing in several counterparts are permitted to the extent legally permissible.

(3) Operational coordination in tickets, pull requests, emails or chat systems may count as text form to the extent the parties use these channels for the project and the content is sufficiently clearly documented.

23. Final provisions

(1) The law of the Federal Republic of Germany applies, excluding the UN Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (CISG).

(2) Place of jurisdiction is the place of jurisdiction specified in the relevant individual agreement, to the extent legally permissible. If no provision is made, the statutory places of jurisdiction apply.

(3) If a provision of these Terms or an individual agreement is or becomes invalid, the validity of the remaining provisions remains unaffected. The parties will replace the invalid provision with a valid provision that comes as close as possible to the economic purpose.

(4) Fovebo may amend these General Terms for future contracts. For existing individual agreements, the version incorporated into that agreement applies unless the parties agree on a new version.

These modules are not automatically part of every engagement. They are attached or referenced where required by the client’s project, data access model or internal procurement/compliance process.

  • Data Access BoundariesProject-specific record of systems, roles, data categories and access limits where needed.
  • Data Processing Agreement / DPAAdditional data processing terms where Fovebo processes personal data on behalf of the Client.
  • Third-country transfer / SCC readinessTransfer documentation and SCC readiness topics where third-country access requires them.
  • Offboarding & Deletion ConfirmationPractical confirmation of access removal, data handling and handover at project end or when access changes.
